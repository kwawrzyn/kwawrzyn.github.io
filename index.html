Past Projects

Developing a Visualization Method with Optical Imaging Data

I developed a method to detect & visualize the electric field effects on biological tissues using biomedical optical imaging. I collected time-series imaging data from an OCT system, used MATLAB for digital signal processing by applying sgolay detrending & Fourier transform to clean & quantify data, codified algorithms to reconstruct digital images, and analyzed data using linear regression to describe the relationship between variables. The work resulted in a new tool, allowing researchers to visualize the morphological and mechanical responses of tissues. The completed project was presented and published at scientific conferences and journals.

A New MRI Pulse Sequence Method for Lung Imaging

I demonstrated the viability of a new MRI method by programmatically modifying, testing, troubleshooting and calibrating a novel MRI sequence for lung imaging. I setup equipment and operated an MRI scanner to collect raw data. MATLAB was used to codify the data analysis routines. Data was processed in both the time and frequency domains. Improvements to SNR and visual appearance of images was accomplished through data cleaning. Results were quantified using Fourier analysis and statistics software such as Prism GraphPad.
